## Funksmith Changelog

### **Verse 1, Line 5**
**Before:** "Dust on my boots and doubt in my chest"  
**After:** "Dust on my boots / Doubt in my chest"

**Why:** Breaks the line into two fragments for breath space. At 95 BPM, this phrasing sits better in the pocket and lets the singer *feel* the weight of each image separately. The slash indicates a breath point—natural and conversational.

---

### **Verse 2, Line 4**
**Before:** "Didn't think that I could stand"  
**After:** "Didn't think my legs could stand"

**Why:** More concrete and physiological. "My legs" grounds the moment in the body—the singer can *feel* this line when performing it. Also removes the slightly awkward "that I" construction, making it more singable and natural.

---

### **Pre-Chorus, Line 3 (New)**
**Added:** "(Who am I?)" after first Pre-Chorus  
**Added:** "(I know now)" after second Pre-Chorus

**Why:** These are *vocal texture cues*, not lyrics to sing. They indicate the emotional/physiological shift in the singer's delivery. First one is questioning, uncertain—voice might crack or waver. Second one is resolved, grounded. This micro-dynamic helps the singer understand the arc and gives the listener a sense of transformation without adding words.

---

### **Bridge, Line 5 (New)**
**Added:** "(That I'm enough)" as a whispered callback

**Why:** The bridge is the vulnerability peak. This parenthetical is the *core realization*—the thing the character finally believes. It's not sung loudly; it's intimate, almost to themselves. Physiologically, this is where the breath catches, where the voice gets smaller before the final chorus explosion. It anchors the emotional journey.

---

### **Overall Phrasing Refinements**

**Micro-dynamics added to arrangement cues:**
- Verse 1: "vocal sits back in mix" — creates space, lets the story breathe
- Pre-Chorus: "lean into the question" — physical cue for the singer to shift energy
- Chorus (2nd repeat): "slightly more powerful" — builds momentum into bridge
- Bridge: "space between phrases" — indicates silence/breath, not rushed delivery

**Why:** These cues help the singer understand where to *hold back* and where to *push forward*. At 95 BPM, this conversational Southern Rock groove needs dynamic contrast. Without it, the song flattens.
